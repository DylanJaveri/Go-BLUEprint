Consulting

Area of Interest Summary

Consulting is a field where experts help individuals or organizations solve problems. A consultant uses specialized knowledge to advise on diverse challenges—whether in management, technology, or strategy—to make businesses more efficient, effective, or better prepared for change. Management or tech consultants, for instance, focus on helping companies address specific business issues, from enhancing technology infrastructure to streamlining workflows or entering new markets.

  • Consulting can seem broad, but it essentially involves experts offering tailored solutions to improve a company's operations. Consulting careers are rewarding, offering the chance to apply expertise meaningfully. While some consultants work in-house, many work externally, especially at large firms like EY, PwC, Deloitte, and KPMG, where they are assigned to various client projects.
  • If you’re exploring consulting careers, a good starting point is to learn about different consulting roles, such as strategy or technology, and take a quiz to discover which path might suit you best.
  • https://www.theforage.com/blog/careers/consulting-careers
Main Questions to consider:
  1. Do I enjoy problem-solving and thrive in fast-paced, dynamic environments?
  2. Am I comfortable working with clients, adapting to their needs, and managing expectations?
  3. Do I have strong analytical, strategic thinking, and communication skills?
  4. Am I willing to work long hours and manage the demands of travel or frequent project changes?
  5. Does the idea of working across diverse industries and continuously learning excite me?
  6. What specific type of consulting aligns with my strengths and interests (e.g., strategy, technology, management)?

Resume Breakdown

  • Initiative: Able to accomplish complex projects and tasks with minimal assistance
  • Leadership/Management: Lead and motivate people from different departments, and provide visionary creative leadership
  • Analytical and Strategic-thinking: Sort through and identify critical data, understand and correctly interpret it, and apply it effectively to drive business growth; able to grasp the big picture and think long-term
  • Quantitative Analysis: Skilled in using quantitative analysis to understand business issues
  • Teamwork: Work in teams consisting of people with skills other than your own
  • Creativity/Innovation: Approach a problem from a different angle, find a better way of doing things, and generate unique ideas

COMBINATION OF THESE AREAS: Not just one focus on leadership, etc.

Interview Overview

  1. Why this company?
  2. Why consulting?
  3. Where do you see yourself in 5-10 years?
  4. Tell us about a time you took initiative / were a leader.
  5. Tell us about a time when you resolved a difficult situation through effective communication. How did you handle it?
  6. Tell us about a time when a collaboration didn’t go well. What was the issue and what would you do differently?
  7. Tell us about a time when you were creative. What did you learn?
  8. Tell us about a time you failed. What did you learn?
  9. Tell us about a time you were on a team and struggled to collaborate well.
  1. Describe a time when a client (or teammate) was unhappy with your work. How did you handle it?
  2. What would you do if a client asked you for advice on an area outside of your expertise?
  3. Tell me about a time when you were faced with a complex problem. How did you go about solving it?
  4. How would you handle a situation where the client’s requested solution doesn’t align with the best long-term interests of their company?

  1. Use Glassdoor to research common interview questions for the specific company.
  2. Make small talk in the beginning of the interview to set a more casual, relaxed tone.
  3. Use STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) or ACR (Action, Context, Result) format to structure your answers.
  4. Answer time sweet spot: 1.5-2 minutes.
  5. Be yourself and be social! Marketing roles often want to see that you are a normal, fun, and interesting person. They want to see your personality and what makes you unique.
  6. Always send a thank you email after your interview.
