Marketing

Area of Interest Summary

  • Students interested in pursuing an Entrepreneurial Venture, General Management, or just running their own business may want to consider Marketing. A career in Marketing, which requires many of the same skills as a General Manager, can prepare an individual for a future entrepreneurial experience. Understanding your long-term career objectives may help clarify the advantages of each of these paths.
  • Marketing includes a wide variety of career opportunities which will be discussed later in this guide. Due to the multitude of opportunities, professional titles and specific roles in the field may vary, depending on the organization. For example, at some companies, the title comparable to "Brand Manager" may be "Product Manager," or the "Business Development" position at one place may be comparable to "Sales" at another.
  • Product and Brand Management functions in all industries use General Management and Marketing skills to oversee the development of a product or service throughout the various stages of its life cycle. The job of a Marketer is to essentially keep the product or service alive and thriving in the market.
  • Regardless of the specific marketing position, there are some basic skills and interests Marketing professionals should possess. Marketers ultimately enjoy leading a process from start to finish. Marketing managers 'own' their product or service and essentially run a small business, so entrepreneurial interest is a positive trait. Marketing professionals have higher creative interests than most operating managers, and are effective at communicating with various stakeholders in an organization. They equally enjoy the financials of the business as well as the creative aspects. In addition, marketing careers call for a strong interest in managing people, as many marketing efforts are team-oriented
  • Work Life Balance: Marketing professionals can expect a diverse, dynamic work experience, with new challenges emerging each day. The workload may fluctuate; for example, during marketing plan development, workweeks typically range from 50-70 hours, followed by slower periods -- it all depends on what the product or brand needs. Marketers can typically expect a positive work-life balance as compared to other functions such as Consulting or Banking.
  • The main skills of a good Marketer revolve around analytical ability and creativity. Many students are intimidated by the "creative" aspect of Marketing because many equate it to artistic ability, which is rarely the case. Creativity in Marketing is the ability to use tools and resources provided to satisfy business objectives. For example, if the objective is to increase sales and awareness for a new brand, but there is limited advertising and promotion spend, the Marketer must use creative thinking to solve this problem. In this case, the Marketer can employ retailer-specific promotions to effectively use promotion dollars, and connect with an influencer to maximize advertising dollars.

  • Does the idea of working across diverse industries and continuously learning excite me?
  • What specific type of consulting aligns with my strengths and interests (e.g., strategy, technology, management)?
  • It is much more analytical than people think. While creativity is important, make sure to emphasize your analytical/ data analysis skills as well
  • Networking is key for marketing! Make sure to focus on this a decent amount

Resume Breakdown

  • Initiative: Able to accomplish complex projects and tasks with minimal assistance
  • Leadership/Management: Lead and motivate people from different departments, and provide visionary creative leadership
  • Analytical and Strategic-thinking: Sort through and identify critical data, understand and correctly interpret it, and apply it effectively to drive business growth; able to grasp the big picture and think long-term
  • Quantitative Analysis: Skilled in using quantitative analysis to understand business issues
  • Teamwork: Work in teams consisting of people with skills other than your own
  • Creativity/Innovation: Approach a problem from a different angle, find a better way of doing things, and generate unique ideas

Marketing 300/Floating Core Course Marketing Management

We recommend taking this course Winter term of sophomore year before you begin recruiting. This is a floating core course and therefore must be completed before graduation. This course will teach you Marketing basics and acts as a prerequisite for other Marketing courses.

Marketing 411: Advertising Management

Even if you are not interested in pursuing a career in advertising, this course gives you insight into how to build, evaluate, and execute a successful Marketing campaign. The course curriculum includes valuable knowledge you can use while recruiting.

Marketing 425 : New Product & Innovation Management

This course exposes you to Marketing analytics in the setting of product innovation. You will be tasked with creating a new product and pushing it to market using the 4P's of Marketing. Critical Marketing topics such as % ACV, TDPs, other distribution measures, as well as focus group and launch information, are also included in the course.

Networking

Networking is key for marketing roles
  • Important to reach out to 4-7 people per company!
  • LinkedIn and Apollo.io will be your best friend for this step
  1. Find people on LinkedIn
  2. Once found, use Apollo.io to get their email address
  3. Draft out a coffee chat invitation
  4. If confirmed, send them a google calendar invite.
  • How long have you been with the company?
  • What about __ has made you want to stay for so long?
  • What made you interested in marketing initially?
  • What is the company culture like? ERG/affinity groups? Social groups?
  • What is the day-to-day job like?
  • How do you like living in ___? (I want to live there after grad)
  • Do you work in teams? What is the team dynamics like?
  • Cross functional?
  • How is the work-life balance?
  • Is there mentorship at the company?
  • What makes ___ stand out?
  • Favorite project you have worked on?
  • General advice for the recruiting process?
  • What makes a successful intern/employee?
  • What was the recruitment process like for you?
  • Was the internships you did similar to your full-time role?
  • What types of projects does your firm specialize in?
  • Most important qualities/skills that ___ emphasizes in their interns/employees?
  • Anyone else you would recommend I speak to to continue learning more about the firm?
  • Final advice/words of wisdom?

Interview Overview

  1. Why this company?
  2. Why consulting?
  3. Where do you see yourself in 5-10 years?
  4. Tell us about a time you took initiative / were a leader.
  5. Tell us about a time when you resolved a difficult situation through effective communication. How did you handle it?
  6. Tell us about a time when a collaboration didn’t go well. What was the issue and what would you do differently?
  7. Tell us about a time when you were creative. What did you learn?
  8. Tell us about a time you failed. What did you learn?
  9. Tell us about a time you were on a team and struggled to collaborate well.

  1. Make small talk in the beginning of the interview to set a more casual, relaxed tone
  2. Use STAR (situation, task, action, result) or ACR (action, context, result) format to structure your answers
  3. Answer time sweet spot: 1.5-2 minutes.
  4. Be yourself and be social! Marketing roles often want to see that you are a normal, fun, and interesting person. They want to see your personality and what makes you unique.
  5. Always send a thank you email after your interview.
